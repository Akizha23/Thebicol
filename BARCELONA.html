Barcelona Ruins Park



The site of Barcelona was originally part of Gubat and Bulusan. The town's name was known as Danlog, taken from the name of a local river. The change of name to Barcelona was recommended by a Spanish official who saw some similarities with Barcelona, Catalonia (Spain). On April 16, 1886, it became a pueblo civil. Until 1868, it was part of the parish of St. James the Great of Bulusan. After 1868, it took St. Joseph as its patron saint and celebrates their town fiesta on May 19.

Location

The Barcelona Ruins Park is located in Poblacion Central, Barcelona, Sorsogon.

The park is located along the shoreline of the Pacific Ocean, providing visitors with stunning coastal views. It's a great place to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.



The Barcelona Ruins Park features a notable bench that has become a popular spot for visitors. This bench is located within the park, offering a place for visitors to sit and enjoy the scenic views of the Pacific Ocean and the historical ruins.
